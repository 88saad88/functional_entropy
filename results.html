4 — FKL: forward and reverse

FKL is asymmetric, like all KL divergences. The forward KL \(\mathrm{KL}(\nu^\text{GT} \| \nu^\text{method})\) penalises a method for placing mass in regions the ground truth doesn't — it is sensitive to mode coverage. The reverse KL \(\mathrm{KL}(\nu^\text{method} \| \nu^\text{GT})\) penalises a method for generating trajectories in regions the ground truth hasn't seen — it is sensitive to mode-seeking behaviour and overconfident extrapolation. Together they give a more complete picture than either alone.

Forward KL — mode coverage
Penalises missing mass. If the method doesn't cover a mode the GT has, KL(GT‖method) grows. "Average case" penalty.
Reverse KL — mode seeking
Penalises spurious mass. If the method generates trajectories the GT doesn't explain, KL(method‖GT) grows. "Worst case" on generated paths.

6 — The Petal case: same snapshot, different dynamics

The Petal dataset has eight sinusoidal branches radiating from the origin. At τ = 0.75, TIGON scores slightly better than MSBM on W₂ (0.155 vs 0.160) and clearly better on MMD (0.018 vs 0.057). Based on marginals alone, TIGON wins. FKL says the opposite: MSBM = 13.6, TIGON = 36.5 — a 2.7× difference.

MSBM
W₂ = 0.160 FKL = 13.6 ✓
Trajectories fan into distinct branches — bifurcation captured. W₂ is marginally worse because bundles spread slightly, but the dynamics are correct.
TIGON
W₂ = 0.155 ✓ FKL = 36.5 ✗
Paths converge to the snapshot positions without separating into branches. Snapshot hit; bifurcation missed entirely.

A method can get the destination right without getting the journey right. In biology, the journey — which cells committed to which lineage, when the branching happened — is usually what matters.

7 — Real data: no ground truth, but FKL still reveals structure

On real scRNA-seq data there are no ground-truth trajectories. FKL uses SBIRR as a reference — it explicitly models differentiation as stochastic drift-diffusion, matching established biological understanding. FKL then quantifies how closely each method recovers those dynamics.

The gap: TIGON's ODE trajectories are too smooth — they miss the inherent stochasticity of stem cell differentiation. A researcher relying only on W₂ would pick TIGON as the best method. FKL says otherwise.

8 — What this means

PatternWhat it meansExample
Deterministic methods inflate marginal scores ODE-based methods produce smooth paths that land near snapshot dots but miss stochastic structure. Marginals reward landing; FKL rewards the full path. hESC: TIGON #1 on W₂, mid-ranked on FKL
Unconstrained SB can be deceptive vSB and MSBM connect point clouds without controlling intermediate dynamics — good marginals, implausible transitions. Repressilator: vSB competitive marginals, FKL = 32 vs SBIRR FKL = 13
Structural constraints in SBIRR pay off SBIRR consistently leads on FKL for stochastic datasets. Its iterative reference refinement encodes knowledge about dynamics — and FKL measures exactly whether that knowledge is right. Best FKL on Lotka-Volterra (30.8), Repressilator (13.4)
The bottom line: marginal metrics measure whether a method reconstructs static distributions at fixed timepoints. FKL measures whether a method has learned the actual dynamics of the system. These are different questions — and for trajectory inference, the second is the one that matters.
